Balaji Temple (19kms)

Experience the divine tranquility of Balaji Temple in Valparai. Surrounded by lush greenery, this sacred temple dedicated to Lord Balaji offers a serene retreat for spiritual seekers. Its peaceful ambiance, intricate architecture, and picturesque setting create the perfect blend of devotion and nature, making it a must-visit destination for those seeking solace and cultural enrichment.

Koozhangal River (13kms)

Koozhangal or Koolangal River is one of the most preferred tourist places in Valparai. It was more a stream than a river. The place is famous for its slow-flowing river, and shallow waters are known for its pebble stone bottom amid verdant, grassy surrounds. The freshwater of the river is clean and pure and is ideal for swimming, and relaxing amid lush green environs.

Birla Waterfalls (13kms)

Cradled by beautiful green tea plantations, Birla Falls is formed over the Vellamalai Tunnel River, originates from the springs located in the Grass Hills of the Valparai Ranges and then proceeds to form the perennial Nadumalai River which flows through the town of Valparai.

Uppar Solaiyar Dam (20kms)

The Sholayar Dam is a popular place for nature lovers. A respite from the bustle of city life can be found in the lush, diverse forests that surround the area. The clear water of the reservoir is a heaven for birdwatchers as it attracts a wide range of migratory birds. The area is home to endangered species like the Nilgiri tahr and the lion-tailed macaque.

Home to a different scope of untamed life.

Nirar dam (19kms)

One of the most interesting places to see in Valparai is the Nirar Dam. It is situated 16 km from the hill station of Valparai and the entire region is well-connected through a good network of roads. There is another adjacent dam called the Lower Nirar Dam located inside the jungle.The most thrilling and intriguing part is crossing the 8km long tunnel that connects the Nirar Dam with the Solaiyar Dam.
